Setting Up Paper Claims 

ChiroTouch provides robust functionality for billers to manage paper claims. Billers can submit, print, resubmit, cancel, and manage statuses from an individual Patient or the Practice Ledger.

The paper claims feature allows practices to generate and print insurance claims onto pre-printed CMS 1500 forms. Most information is gathered automatically by the system, and many fields can be updated directly from the form if needed. The system will alert users about required and recommended fields, and will display helpful information about requirements such as timely filing, pre-certification, and chart notes.

Create A Primary Paper Claim

1. To generate a paper claim, the selected payer must be configured to submit paper claims. If you have not already done this:

  • Go to Payer Plan Maintenance
  • Pick the Payer you would like to update
  • From the Update Payer Plan slideout, click on the Claim Submission tab
  • Update the As Primary Payer and/or As Secondary Payer dropdowns to Paper Claims
  • Save your update

2. To start a paper claim, your charges must first be invoiced. If all of the charges are from an encounter, they will be invoiced when they are posted within the encounter. If, for some reason, there are charges that have not been invoiced yet, you will need to make sure they are invoiced before they are added to the claim. Each invoice that is added to the claim must include at least one charge that stems from an encounter. 

From Patient Encounters, you can View Charges using the action menu (ellipsis) for any encounter.

3.  The Charges will open for the invoice you have clicked on.  This is a snapshot of the patient's insurance info and the charges that will be billed.  Now click on the Claims tab.

4.  If you get to the Claims tab and are unable to ADD a claim, check the following:

  • Are these charges billable to insurance?
  • Do you have a primary insurance payer set?

5.  If you do get the Add button, with an insurance payer, you are on the right path.  You can now work the claim through the Ledger.

6.  Once the claim is added, you'll see it.

7.  Adding a claim opens the Add New Claim window, where you can see what the claim will look like.  Review this draft status for any issues, and generate the claim once it is ready to go. Any fields in this window that have the icon (in the upper right corner) are editable directly from this window.

NOTE: If your Add New Claim shows Notifications, Errors, and/or Warnings, see below to learn to Resolve Claim Before Generating.

8. Any fields in this window that have the icon (in the upper right corner of the box) are editable directly from this window.

9.  Once you have reviewed the claim form for any issues and solved them, click Generate. If the claim is generated successfully, a green alert will appear at the top of the screen, and you will have the ability to print the claim.

10. Click PRINT CLAIM in the upper left corner. From here, you can also change the status of the claim to Printed from the dropdown in the upper right corner if you wish. Once the claim has been printed, close the claim window and return to Charges.

11. Now in Charges, you will see the claim and the status listed as Printed.  

12.  This claim is now Ready to Submit.

Resolve Claim Before Generating

1.  When you Add New Claim, you can view the claim form as the information will be submitted to the payer.

  • View required fields, recommended fields, and notifications
  • Save a draft claim
  • Update certain claim fields
  • Generate the claim

2.  If you see notifications, errors, and warnings, you will need to resolve these to properly complete the claim form and increase your chances that it will be accepted the first time.  Follow each link if you need to see more detail about resolving.

  • Errors - any required fields that are not populated will be displayed and the corresponding field on the form will be highlighted with a Required label
  • Warnings- any warnings that are not populated will be displayed and the corresponding field on the form will be highlighted with a Recommended label. Recommended fields are not required by the NUCC guidelines for claim submission, but omitting them may lead to claim rejection depending on the submission requirements of the payer.
  • Notifications- relevant to the claim based on the payer plan settings will be displayed in the collapsible menu.

3. Any fields in this window that have an icon (in the upper right corner of their box) are editable directly from this window. (i.e. Any field with the orange box around the icon.)

Save Draft

A claim draft can be saved if you have started working on the claim, and need to finish it at a later time. To save a claim draft, click SAVE.

NOTE: If there are any errors on the claim form, they will have to be addressed before you can save the claim draft.

Managing Paper Claims

Billers have flexibility when managing paper claims.  A paper claim status can be changed manually once the claim has been generated and no longer displays on Ready to Submit.  This can be done from the Claim form or the Claims Worklist.  These are the statuses available:

  • Generated
  • Printed
  • Mailed
  • Needs Attention
  • Denied
  • Rejected
  • Approved

Change Paper Claim Status

Once a paper claim has been generated, the claim's status can be updated to help you keep track of where the claim is in the claim lifecycle.

For a single paper claim:

  1. Select the patient.
  2. Navigate to the claim form.
  3. Click the down arrow on the dropdown menu (on the right).
  4. Select the new status.

As part of a Bulk Claim:

  1. Select Claims.
  2. Click the action menu (ellipsis).
  3. Select Status.
  4. Select the new status from the dropdown menu and click OK.

Print

A claim must be generated before it can be printed. Printing a claim will open a new browser tab with the printable claim displayed in a PDF format in a layout that matches standard pre-printed CMS 1500 forms. You can use this print preview as a final check to make sure everything looks good before you print the claim.

1. To print the claim:

  • Open a claim that is in a status of Generated
  • Click on the PRINT CLAIM
  • Review the printable PDF form on the newly opened tab
  • Use your browser's print functionality to print the form to the correct printer that contains pre-printed CMS 1500 claim forms

2. Sometimes, depending on the specific model of the printer, the printed form data will not exactly match the layout of pre-printed CMS 1500 form. To address this, you can adjust the printing offsets and re-print the form.

To adjust print offsets:

  • Click on the Adjust Print Offsets link to the right of the Print Claim button to open the Print Settings
  • Adjust the Top Offset and Left Offset values as needed

    NOTE: The values on this dialog are in millimeters. Positive values in the top offset will move the form content down. Negative values will move it up. Positive values in the left offset will move the form content right. Negative values will move it left.

3. Click on the OK button to save your print offset adjustments.

4.  Print the claim again. Ensure that the printed claim data matches the layout of the pre-printed CMS 1500 form. If needed, repeat steps 1 – 4 until the printed claim data matches the layout of the pre-printed CMS 1500 form

Printing Bulk Paper Claims

You have the ability to Bulk Print Claims.

1. Claims that can be printed are those claims that are:

  • Paper with a status of generated
  • E-claim with a status that is not Error or Validation Error can be previewed, using the print icon

2.  Claims may be printed from the Claims Worklist.

3.  Paper Claims that are Bulk Generated, and are missing fields, will not be available to be printed. They will be moved to the Needs Attention to be reviewed. Status will be Validation Error.

4.  The ability to print claim batches will only be available if the claim batch is complete. Print icon will only display for completed paper batches.

All batches can be previewed before printing. 

Secondary Paper Claim

1.  To create a Secondary Claim, the payer must be configured to submit paper claims. To do this:

  • Go to Payer Plan Maintenance
  • Click the Payer you would like to update
  • From the Update Payer Plan slideout, click on the Claim Submission
  • Update the As Primary Payer and/or As Secondary Payer dropdowns to Paper Claims
  • Save your update

2. Open an invoiced charge group from the patient or practice ledger OR Open an un-invoiced charge group.  Select the charge(s) that you want to add to an invoice.  And Click on Create Invoice.

3. Ensure that the Bill Type is set to Insurance and that there is an insurance plan selected from the “Plan” dropdown. 
4. Ensure that there is at least one encounter charge on the invoice.

5. Enable at least one charge using the “COB” checkbox, and a “Secondary Plan” selected. To do this:

  • Select the charge(s) that you want to edit from the charges table at the bottom of the overlay
  • Click on the Edit Charges button
  • Enable the COB checkbox
  • Select a plan from the Secondary Plan dropdown (it will default to the secondary plan configured for the charge group, but can be updated to any other secondary plans configured for the patient)
  • Click on the Save.

6. Click on Claims.

7. Click on ADD.

8. Select the Add Secondary Claim option for the appropriate payer to open the Add New Claim window.
NOTE: If there are multiple secondary payers configured for the charges in the charge group, each payer will be listed in this menu, and you can select the payer that you want to submit the claim for.
