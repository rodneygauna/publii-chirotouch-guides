A

Action Menua dropdown menu of all of the actions that can be performed in a particular area of ChiroTouch (see visual list of icons) 
Active Guarantorsa list of the guarantors who are active in the system for a particular patient
Acutesevere and sudden onset
Alertimportant information about the patient that appears at the top of the screen in a red box; users should always be aware of this information when they are interacting with a patient
Aliasnickname given to an insurance payer (only seen in the ChiroTouch system) to help you quickly differentiate payers; especially helpful if they have the same name
Allocatedpayments that have been connected to a specific charge in the billing system 
Allowed Amountthe maximum amount of money that a patient’s insurance allows the practice to charge the patient for a particular item or service
Appointment Typedifferent classifications of appointments that inform the provider or specialist of the reason for the patient’s visit (e.g., massage, new patient visit). Appointment types are specific to each practice and are defined by the practice’s users
Arrowan icon that opens up a menu of more options or gives access to the full amount of information when only part of it is on the screen
Assessmenta summary of significant data in a patient’s chart, along with any provider conclusions
Auto SALT (Automatic Same As Last Treatment) - information that is automatically added to a patient’s chart upon its opening, taken from the patient’s last visit
Available for Purchasean item that a patient can purchase outside of an encounter (e.g., supplements, Therabands)

B

Batchesforms produced for multiple patients through one action (as opposed to creating the form individually for each patient)

C

Capitated Fee Schedulea breakdown of what a managed care organization pays for services for its members to the health care provider
Caregivera person who cares for a patient outside of a medical practice; one of the choices for a guarantor’s relationship to the patient
Casea classification of care that allows providers to keep track of information about an issue with a patient (e.g., onset dates, hospitalization dates) to help with billing and insurance claims
Checkboxesboxes that can be selected (checked) in order to choose one or more of a group of options
Chronic
require ongoing medical attention or limit activities of daily living or both
Claim Batch
a group of claims that are produced together, all at the same time 
Claims Worklisttabs in ChiroTouch (available from the patient account or the Practice Ledger) that provide a global view of all of the insurance claims associated with the patient or the practice
COBCoordination of Benefits
Copaythe amount of money that is the patient’s responsibility before claims are sent to the insurance company

D

Dashboardthe area of ChiroTouch where an overarching view of the practice’s financial and care activities are summarized
Deductiblethe fixed amount or percentage of charges that a patient must pay for before insurance will begin to enact coverage. For example, if the patient is charged $1500 for a service and has a $500 deductible, the patient is responsible for the first $500 and insurance will cover all or part of the remaining $1000.
Dependenta person who X; one of the choices for a guarantor’s relationship to the patient
Diagnosisa conclusion that the provider makes about what is causing a patient’s issues; diagnoses are linked to care actions and provide justification for insurance billing
Diagnosis Pointera field in the Charges section of the patient’s chart that allows the provider to connect a charge to a specific diagnosis; this is often required for billing
Documentsa section of a patient’s account, also viewable in their chart, where any documents (e.g., x-rays, HIPAA permissions, forms) can be stored electronically
DOSthe acronym for Date of Service
Durable Medical Equipment (DME):medical equipment, such as braces, that can be reused over a long period of time
DXan abbreviation for diagnosis

E

EasyTouchone of two sets of charting macros that are included in ChiroTouch 
Electronic Claimsclaims that are submitted to the insurance company through electronic means, usually through the Clearinghouse 
Encountera time when the patient meets with the provider and care is given; encounters provide the basis for charting
EOB(Explanation of Benefits) a form that is received from the insurance company, often with a payment, that explains which patients and charges the payment is connected to 
EPSDTEarly and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic and Treatment 
ERA(Electronic Remittance Advice) an explanation from a health plan to a provider about a claim payment

F

Fee Schedulea complete listing of fees used by Medicare to pay doctors or other providers/suppliers.  This comprehensive listing of fee maximums is used to reimburse a physician and/or other providers on a fee-for-service basis
Fee Sch ChrgFee Schedule Charge - use to override the base amount of a charge

G

Gradualan adjective that describes the onset of symptoms over a period of time, not due to one triggering event
Group IDan identification number on an insurance card associated with the group through which a patient maintains their insurance coverage
Guarantora person who agrees to pay debts owed to the practice if the patient does not

H

HIPAAthe Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, a federal law that governs how personal health information is handled

I

ICD10 Codescodes that are set under HIPAA to standardize diagnoses across the medical field for ease of record-keeping 
Individual NPIsee NPI Type 1 
Insured IDthe number on an insurance card associated with the policyholder 
Invoicea list of all the charges for one or more encounters for an individual patient 

L

Ledgeran area of the software where financial information is summarized (see also: Patient Ledger, Practice Ledger) 

M

Macroa set of instructions within charting that allow the provider to take notes about a patient’s care without having to write out the entire note. Each macro provides the general language around which the provider can make appropriate specific descriptive choices from a menu 
MOImechanism of injury

MRN 

(Medical Record Number)

a unique identifier assigned to each patient when their patient account is created. MRNs can be auto-generated by ChiroTouch or created by the user, and they can be modified at any time 

N

NDCNational Drug Code - a unique, 3-segment numeric identifier assigned to each medication listed under Section 510 of the U.S. Federal Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act 
Noteinformation about a patient that users should be aware of, but that does not rise to the level of an Alert (see also: Alert)
NPIthe acronym for National Provider Identifier; NPI numbers allow providers to follow HIPAA guidelines, improving the efficiency and effectiveness of electronic transmission of health information
NPI Type 1  (Individual)an NPI for an individual provider
NPI Type 2 (Organization)an NPI for an organization 
Networka group of practitioners who have agreed to work with a particular insurance company 
  • In-Network: an adjective used to describe services that are provided by a practitioner in an insurance company’s network 
  • Out-of-Network: an adjective used to describe services that are provided by a practitioner who is not in an insurance company’s network 
NTE(this is in FL 19 under payer plans)

O

Objective (data)information in a patient’s chart that is obtained by the provider through physical examination of the patient and laboratory data 
Out of Pocket charges that are the patient’s responsibility
Overridean option in the New Appointment slideout that allows the scheduler to create an appointment that may violate scheduling rules and provider availability

P

Paper Claimsclaims that are submitted to the insurance company via a paper copy that is sent through the mail
Patient Accountthe page in ChiroTouch where all of the information about a particular patient can be accessed
Patient Ledgerthe page in ChiroTouch that provides an overview of all of the financial records of a particular patient 
Plan (charting)intended diagnostic or therapeutic action
Plan IDan identification number associated with an insurance plan; (see also: Group ID)
Policy #the number on an insurance card associated with the policyholder 
Policyholderthe person in whose name insurance is maintained. For many people, this is “self;” others are covered under a spouse’s or parent’s insurance. If the patient is not covered under a policy in their own name, the policyholder is the person whose name is on the insurance policy
Practice Addressthe physical address of the practice
Practice Ledgerthe page in ChiroTouch that provides the user with a global overview of all of the practice’s patient billing and insurance activities 
Problem Lista list of all of the known issues that a patient has that might affect decisions about care, whether or not the provider is actively providing care for those issues
Proc (Ledger)abbreviation for Procedure 
Providera user who provides care to patients and has a NPI number. Providers can sign encounters and bill insurance.
PWK(this is in FL 19 under payer plans)

R

Radio buttonssmall buttons that you can click on to choose one option from among a group
REF(this is in FL 19 under payer plans)
Referring Providerthe practitioner who referred the patient for chiropractic care
Rendering Providerthe provider or specialist who met with the patient during their encounter

S

(S)
an abbreviation for Secondary that appears to the left of the payer when the invoice is for a secondary insurance
Self-limiting
a disease process that resolves spontaneously with or without specific treatment
SOAPthe acronym for “Subjective (data), Objective (data), Assessment, Plan;” data that are used when charting an encounter (see also: definitions of each data type) 
SOFthe acronym for “signature on file”
Specialista user who provides care to patients but does not have a NPI number. Specialists can sign encounters but cannot bill insurance
Staffusers who are not directly involved with patient care (e.g., scheduling, front desk, billing). Staff cannot sign encounters
Statementa list of all of the charges that the patient is responsible for paying after insurance has processed claims (if applicable)
Subjective (data)information in a patient’s chart that was provided by the patient or their family
Subscriber IDthe number on an insurance card associated with the patient, if the patient is not the policyholder
Super Usera user who needs access to all global functions in the practice, including the ability to manage additional users
Superbill a list of all charges paid by the patient that can be submitted to the patient’s insurance for reimbursement

T

Timed Procedurea procedure that is billed in increments of time
Treatment Plana plan set forth by the provider for the number and frequency of additional appointments that a patient should follow for proper treatment
Txan abbreviation for Treatment

U

Unallocatedan adjective to describe payments that have not yet been connected to a particular charge within a patient’s account

W

WNLwithin normal limits
Write Offthe difference between the amount that is normally charged by the practice for a particular service and what the insurance company will allow the practice to charge them

X

X-ray Datethe date on which the last x-ray was performed
